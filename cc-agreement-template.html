02What we'll do

Services

2.1 Scope

The Consultant will provide the services described in the accompanying Proposal dated [Proposal date], reference [CC-XXXX] ("the Services"). The Proposal is incorporated into this Agreement by reference and, together with this document, forms the complete agreement between the parties.

Where the terms of the Proposal and this Agreement conflict, this Agreement takes precedence on matters of legal interpretation; the Proposal takes precedence on matters of scope, timeline, and price.

2.2 Changes to scope

Either party may request changes to the Services. A change takes effect only when both parties agree to it in writing (email is sufficient). The Consultant will estimate any impact on fees or timeline before work on the change begins.

2.3 Standard of work

The Consultant will carry out the Services with reasonable skill and care, in line with accepted standards for professional consultancy work in the UK. The Consultant does not guarantee any specific commercial outcome — consultancy is advisory, not a warranty of business results.

2.4 Client responsibilities

The Client will:

  • Provide timely access to the people, data, and systems needed to deliver the Services.
  • Nominate a single point of contact authorised to make decisions on the Client's behalf.
  • Review and respond to requests for input, feedback, or approval within five working days, unless another period is agreed.
  • Flag any issues, concerns, or changes in circumstance as early as possible.

Where the Client's delay or non-response causes the Consultant to be unable to progress the Services, the Consultant may pause work and adjust the timeline accordingly, without this constituting a breach of this Agreement.

03What it costs and how it's paid

Fees & payment

3.1 Fees

The fees for the Services are set out in the Proposal. All fees are stated in Pounds Sterling and exclude VAT, which will be added at the prevailing rate if applicable.

3.2 Payment model

The Services will be invoiced on one of the following bases, as specified in the Proposal:

  • Fixed fee, milestone-based: 50% of each phase invoiced on phase commencement; the remaining 50% invoiced on phase completion.
  • Monthly retainer: a fixed monthly fee, invoiced on the first working day of each month, covering the scope of services set out in the Proposal.
  • Time-based (hourly or day rate): invoiced monthly in arrears, with a written statement of time spent. Where a cap is specified in the Proposal, the Consultant will not exceed the cap without prior written approval.

3.3 Payment terms

  • Invoices are payable within 48 hours of the invoice date.
  • Payment is made by BACS transfer to the account detailed on the invoice.
  • Travel and pre-agreed third-party costs (software, licences, subcontractor fees) are billed at cost on production of receipts.
  • Late payment: statutory interest under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 may be applied to overdue amounts.
  • If an invoice is overdue by more than 7 days, the Consultant may suspend the Services until the invoice is paid, without this constituting a breach of this Agreement.

3.4 Expenses

Expenses directly incurred in delivering the Services (pre-agreed travel, specialist software licences, third-party research reports, etc.) will be reimbursed at cost. Expenses above £100 per item require the Client's prior written approval.

04Who owns what

Intellectual property

4.1 Background IP

Each party retains ownership of any intellectual property, know-how, frameworks, tools, templates, or methodologies that it owned or developed before this Agreement, or that it develops independently of the Services ("Background IP"). Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of a party's Background IP to the other party.

4.2 Deliverables

"Deliverables" means the specific final outputs produced for the Client under the Services — for example, reports, strategy documents, roadmaps, designs, or written recommendations, as set out in the Proposal.

On receipt of full payment of the fees relating to a Deliverable, the Consultant assigns ownership of that Deliverable to the Client. The Client may thereafter use, adapt, publish, and distribute the Deliverable as it sees fit within its own business.

Before full payment is received, the Consultant retains ownership of all Deliverables, and grants the Client a limited licence to use them for internal review purposes only.

4.3 Consultant's retained rights

The Consultant retains a permanent, non-exclusive right to:

  • Use the name and logo of the Client, and reference the engagement at a high level, in its portfolio, case studies, website, and new-business materials.
  • Reuse any methodology, framework, or internal working document developed during the engagement in future work for other clients, provided it does not contain the Client's Confidential Information.

Where the Client has a legitimate reason to restrict portfolio use (e.g. commercial sensitivity, competitor considerations), this should be flagged in writing before signing, and the parties will agree a reasonable alternative — such as anonymised case-study references.

4.4 Third-party IP

Where the Services include or rely on third-party intellectual property (stock images, licensed software, templates), the Consultant will disclose this to the Client, and any associated licence terms apply to the Client's use.

05Keeping things private

Confidentiality

5.1 What counts as confidential

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information that one party shares with the other in connection with the Services — including financial data, customer lists, strategy, product plans, technical information, staff information, and any document or communication marked or reasonably understood as confidential.

5.2 Obligations

Each party will:

  • Keep the other party's Confidential Information private and secure.
  • Use it only for the purpose of delivering or benefiting from the Services.
  • Share it only with those of its personnel or advisers who need to see it, and who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.
  • Not disclose it to any third party without the other party's prior written consent.

5.3 Exceptions

The obligations in 5.2 do not apply to information that is or becomes public through no fault of the receiving party, was already known to the receiving party before disclosure, is independently developed by the receiving party, or is required to be disclosed by law, regulator, or court order (in which case the receiving party will notify the disclosing party promptly, where legally permitted to do so).

5.4 Duration

The confidentiality obligations in this clause continue for three years after this Agreement ends, except for information that is a trade secret, which remains confidential indefinitely.

5.5 Data protection

Both parties will comply with applicable UK data protection law (UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018) when handling any personal data shared in connection with the Services. Where the Services involve the Consultant processing personal data on behalf of the Client, the parties will enter into a separate Data Processing Agreement if required.

06Ending the engagement

Termination

6.1 Phase-gated termination

This Agreement operates on a phase-gated basis, matching the phase structure of the Proposal. Neither party may terminate this Agreement for convenience during an active phase of work.

At the end of each phase, either party may give written notice that it does not wish to proceed to the next phase. In that case, this Agreement ends on completion of the current phase, with fees owing up to and including that phase.

6.2 Termination for cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately, at any time, by written notice if the other party:

  • Commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to remedy it within 14 days of written notice to do so.
  • Becomes insolvent, enters administration, or is otherwise unable to pay its debts as they fall due.
  • Behaves in a way that makes continued working relationship unworkable (e.g. abusive conduct, harassment of personnel, requests to act illegally or unethically).

6.3 Effect of termination

On termination, for any reason:

  • The Client pays for all Services delivered up to the effective date of termination, and for any non-cancellable costs already committed.
  • Each party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information on request.
  • The IP, confidentiality, liability, and general clauses continue to apply after termination.
07Limits of responsibility

Liability

7.1 Cap on liability

Except for the exclusions in clause 7.3, the Consultant's total liability to the Client arising out of or in connection with this Agreement — whether in contract, tort, negligence, or otherwise — is capped at the total fees paid by the Client to the Consultant under this Agreement in the twelve months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

7.2 Excluded losses

The Consultant is not liable for:

  • Loss of profit, revenue, or anticipated savings.
  • Loss of business opportunity or goodwill.
  • Loss or corruption of data, where such data has not been provided to the Consultant under appropriate backup arrangements.
  • Indirect, consequential, or special losses, however arising.

7.3 What cannot be limited

Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits liability for:

  • Death or personal injury caused by negligence.
  • Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation.
  • Any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by law.

7.4 Insurance

The Consultant maintains professional indemnity insurance appropriate to the nature and scale of the Services. Details are available on request.

08Everything else

General

8.1 Independent contractor

The Consultant provides the Services as an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the parties.

8.2 Subcontracting

The Consultant may engage subcontractors to assist in delivering the Services, provided the Consultant remains responsible for the overall quality of the work and for the subcontractor's compliance with this Agreement's confidentiality and IP obligations.

8.3 Non-solicitation

Neither party will, during the term of this Agreement and for six months afterwards, directly solicit for employment any individual introduced to them through the Services, without the other party's prior written consent. This does not restrict general recruitment advertising not specifically targeted at the other party's personnel.

8.4 Notices

Any notice under this Agreement must be in writing and sent by email to the addresses given in clause 1 (or any updated address notified in writing). Notices by email are deemed received at the time of sending, unless an out-of-office or bounce response is received.

8.5 Entire agreement

This Agreement and the Proposal form the entire agreement between the parties on the subject matter. They supersede any previous discussions, representations, or understandings.

8.6 Variation

Any variation to this Agreement must be agreed in writing by both parties (email is sufficient).

8.7 Waiver

A failure or delay by either party in enforcing a right under this Agreement does not waive that right.

8.8 Severability

If any clause of this Agreement is held by a court to be unlawful or unenforceable, the rest of the Agreement continues in force. The parties will negotiate in good faith to replace the affected clause with one that gives effect to the original commercial intention.

8.9 Governing law and jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by the laws of Scotland. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Scottish courts, except that the Consultant may bring proceedings for unpaid fees in any court of competent jurisdiction.
