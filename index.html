Three projects from the last 18 months representing the types of problems solved and how. Each one is running in production today. Client names are illustrative of sector rather than disclosure.

Case 01
Commercial kitchen extraction & HVAC installer

BESA-certified specialist serving London hospitality. 20-year operator, complex multi-stakeholder proposals, CRM and outreach sprawl.

Monday.comMake.comCloudflare Pages Claude APIGoogle AdsLooker Studio

From spreadsheet chaos to a proposal factory — and a sales pipeline that tells the truth.

Proposals in Word. Deals tracked across four separate Monday boards that didn't talk to each other. Google Ads burning cash against mismatched keywords. Nothing was broken enough to stop the business — but the cost of running it was quietly eating owner time and margin.

The rebuild came in layers: a three-template HTML proposal generator on Cloudflare Pages producing branded PDFs in under two minutes, a restructured Monday.com architecture across five boards with standardised statuses and automations, a Make.com scenario syncing Gmail quotes into Google Sheets and Xero, and three rebuilt Google Ads campaigns with Looker Studio dashboards feeding weekly owner reports. Service agreements now sign in a day instead of a week, with weekly pipeline truth instead of monthly surprises.

Claude APIDiscovery analysisCRM notes Google AdsSEO reporting

05 — Approach Process

How engagements actually run.

Five phases, no surprises. Every project follows the same discipline — whether it's a two-week audit or a three-month phased build. Transparency about process is as important as transparency about price.

I

Listen

Structured discovery call to learn how the business actually runs — not how the org chart says it does. Existing tools, pain points, the manual hacks everyone pretends aren't there.

II

Map

A short, blunt opportunity map. What to automate, what to leave alone, where AI pays back, where it's noise. You see exactly what's being proposed and why — before committing to build.

III

Build

Phased delivery with working systems at each stage gate. You see value before you commit to the next phase. Every integration is documented, every automation has a kill switch.

IV

Hand over

Knowledge transfer, not goodbye. Run-books documented, training delivered to the people who'll use it, 30-day transitional support. Your team owns everything built — you could run it alone from here if you wanted to.

V

Partner

Most clients continue together. Monthly-rolling partnership where we handle orchestration, platform updates, new automations, and new integrations — while you retain ownership of everything built. Operational capacity, plus an eye on what's changing in the tools layer.
