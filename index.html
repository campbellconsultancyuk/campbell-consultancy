02 — Selected work

Real systems, shipped.

Three projects from the last 18 months representing the types of problems solved and how. Each one is running in production today. Client names are illustrative of sector rather than disclosure.

Case 01
Commercial kitchen extraction & HVAC installer
BESA-certified specialist serving London hospitality. 20-year operator, complex multi-stakeholder proposals, CRM and outreach sprawl.
Monday.com Make.com Cloudflare Pages Claude API Google Ads Looker Studio

From spreadsheet chaos to a proposal factory — and a sales pipeline that tells the truth.

Proposals in Word. Deals tracked across four separate Monday boards that didn't talk to each other. Google Ads burning cash against mismatched keywords. Nothing was broken enough to stop the business — but the cost of running it was quietly eating owner time and margin.

The rebuild came in layers: a three-template HTML proposal generator on Cloudflare Pages producing branded PDFs in under two minutes, a restructured Monday.com architecture across five boards with standardised statuses and automations, a Make.com scenario syncing Gmail quotes into Google Sheets and Xero, and three rebuilt Google Ads campaigns with Looker Studio dashboards feeding weekly owner reports. Service agreements now sign in a day instead of a week, with weekly pipeline truth instead of monthly surprises.

Outcomes
  • <2 min Quote to branded PDF, from weeks
  • 4→1 CRM systems consolidated into Monday
  • 496 Hospitality contacts segmented & outreach-ready
  • 3 Ads campaigns rebuilt, negatives applied, monitored
Case 02
Multi-site London restaurant group onboarding
Four-site hospitality group onboarded onto a bi-annual cleaning service agreement. Signed service agreement, branded quote, CRM setup, calendar automation.
Monday.com WeasyPrint Google Calendar API Xero Docx automation

Signed, scheduled and invoiced — end-to-end, inside 48 hours.

A new four-site restaurant client came in with tight timelines and a single operations director as primary contact. Traditionally this means a week of back-and-forth on quotes, agreement drafting, calendar coordination, and manual CRM entries across multiple boards.

Instead, the full onboarding ran through a templated quote generator producing branded DOCX and PDF service agreements, automated Monday.com entries across four boards, and Google Calendar events with per-site scheduling pushed into the correct operations calendar — all from one structured intake. The account manager picked up a complete handover: signed, scheduled, bi-annual recurrence built in. Client saw one clean email thread.

Outcomes
  • 48h Initial contact to signed & scheduled
  • 4 sites Coordinated without scheduling conflicts
  • 0 Manual CRM entries required from account manager
  • Template Flow reusable for every subsequent multi-site client
Case 03
Digital marketing agency · client reporting pipeline
AU/UK-based digital agency serving financial advisors, immigration firms, video production houses and trade businesses. Strategy, reporting, onboarding.
Claude API Discovery analysis CRM notes Google Ads SEO reporting

Turning discovery-call noise into client-ready strategy, monthly.

Every agency has the same problem: a rich discovery call that then evaporates into a Zoom recording nobody re-watches. By the time delivery starts, nuance is lost, briefs are generic, and the client wonders why you didn't hear them.

A discovery-transcript analysis pipeline takes a raw call transcript and produces three deliverables in one pass — a <2,500-character structured CRM note, a client summary email written in the agency's voice, and an internal strategy brief. The same pattern runs for monthly performance reports: raw analytics in, narrative client-ready report out, drafted by AI, reviewed by humans. Across a 10+ client portfolio, monthly reporting admin dropped from a day per client to twenty minutes of review.

Outcomes
  • ~20 min Per monthly report, from a full day
  • 3-in-1 CRM note, client email, internal brief from one transcript
  • Voice-matched Every draft tuned to the agency's house style
  • Repeatable Same pattern applied across 10+ active accounts

05 — Approach

How engagements actually run.

Four phases, no surprises. Every project follows the same discipline — whether it's a two-week audit or a three-month phased build. Transparency about process is as important as transparency about price.

I

Listen

Structured discovery call to learn how the business actually runs — not how the org chart says it does. Existing tools, pain points, the manual hacks everyone pretends aren't there.

II

Map

A short, blunt opportunity map. What to automate, what to leave alone, where AI pays back, where it's noise. You see exactly what's being proposed and why — before committing to build.

III

Build

Phased delivery with working systems at each stage gate. You see value before you commit to the next phase. Every integration is documented, every automation has a kill switch.

IV

Hand over

Your team owns what's been built. Run-books documented, training delivered to the people who'll use it, and 30 days of post-launch support. No lock-in, no black boxes.
