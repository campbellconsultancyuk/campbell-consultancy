Executive summary

The aim is simple: reduce the time you and your managers spend on HR, onboarding and payroll admin, and give you weekly visibility over labour cost as a percentage of revenue — all without ripping out tools that already work.

The good news is that the two systems you use — BreatheHR and Xero Payroll — already talk to each other natively. Employee data syncs from Breathe to Xero automatically, and payslips pull back into staff Breathe profiles once payroll runs. That removes roughly 60% of the plumbing we would otherwise have to build.

The real opportunity is therefore not in replacing these systems but in the three gaps around them: (1) getting clean, approved timesheet hours into Xero without manual re-entry, (2) giving staff a self-service channel for the questions that currently interrupt managers, and (3) giving you a weekly labour snapshot per site so there are no end-of-month surprises.

Bottom line

A meaningful first version can go live in 3–4 weeks for under £1,200. A full three-site build lands in the £2,800–£5,800 range depending on scope. Most three-site operations then move onto an ongoing partnership at £500/month to keep the system tuned, monitored, and extending — 30-day rolling, no lock-in.

Where the time goes today

Before recommending anything, it's worth naming the specific admin patterns that eat the most time in a typical three-site hospitality operation. These are the targets.

Admin task Typical time per week
New starter onboarding (paperwork, right-to-work, bank details, handbook issue) 45–90 mins per starter
Fielding staff questions (payslip queries, holiday balance, rota clarification) 3–5 hours per manager
Collecting and approving timesheets before payroll runs 2–4 hours per site
Compiling labour cost reports after payroll has run (too late to act) 2–3 hours + frustration
Contract generation, signing and filing 30–60 mins per staff change
Chasing missing documents, policy acknowledgements, training records 1–2 hours per week

Across three sites, that's realistically 20–30 hours of manager and owner time per week that is not going into food, guests, or growth. The entire case for automation is recovering most of that time, predictably, with controls you trust.

How the automated system fits together

The target system has five layers. Only the middle layer — the orchestration and AI — is new work. Everything else is already in place or is a well-trodden off-the-shelf choice.

Layer 1 — Your existing systems (unchanged)

  • BreatheHR — remains the single source of truth for employee records, contracts, holiday balances, sickness, and documents.
  • Xero Payroll — continues to handle RTI submissions, pension auto-enrolment (NEST or similar), tax calculations, and HMRC compliance. We touch none of the compliance plumbing — that's Xero's job and they do it well.
  • The native Breathe ↔ Xero integration stays switched on. Employee data, pay changes and leaver information flow automatically between them at no cost.

Layer 2 — Rota and timesheet capture

This is the one area where a platform decision matters. Hours worked need to get from shift floor into Xero cleanly and accurately — it's the single biggest source of payroll error in hospitality.

If a rota system is already in place, we integrate with it. If not, my recommendation is RosterElf for a three-site operation — it's UK-built, purpose-designed for Xero Payroll UK, and applies Working Time Regulation rules before timesheets reach Xero. Realistic monthly cost: around £50–80 total across three sites.

Layer 3 — The automation & AI layer (the new bit)

This is what I build. It sits above the existing tools and orchestrates them. The core engine is Make.com — a visual automation platform that runs scheduled and triggered workflows between systems. On top of that, specific AI capabilities are added where they earn their keep.

  • Onboarding automation: new starter form → BreatheHR record → right-to-work docs filed in Drive → welcome pack sent → manager notified.
  • AI staff assistant: WhatsApp bot using Claude or GPT that answers staff questions using real data pulled live from Breathe and Xero — never guessed.
  • Weekly labour snapshot: every Monday morning, an email with total labour cost, labour % of revenue, overtime spend, and site-by-site variance vs budget.
  • Exception alerts: missing timesheets, pending right-to-work expiry, holiday allowance anomalies, overtime thresholds breached.

Layer 4 — Staff interface

Staff interact through WhatsApp (for questions) and a simple branded onboarding web form (Typeform or Tally). No new app to download, no new login for them to remember. Managers get exception alerts and labour reports by email and optional WhatsApp.

Layer 5 — Your dashboard (optional, phase 2)

If useful, a simple owner dashboard can be built in Retool or a similar low-code tool showing labour cost per site in near real-time. This is not required for phase 1 — the weekly snapshot email covers most of the need.

Four delivery options

Option 4 · Ongoing partnership (recommended)

Build it, then keep improving it

Any of Options 1–3, paired with a monthly partnership once the system is live. This is the option most suited to a three-site operation where the stakes are high and the cost of a silent failure (missed payroll, broken onboarding, compliance drift) is much greater than the cost of having someone actively looking after it.

The build doesn't stop being valuable on day one — it keeps compounding as we tune it, extend it, and adapt it to seasonal patterns. This option formalises that work and makes the outcomes predictable.

What's included every month

  • System health monitoring — automations are actively watched. API changes, rate limits, authentication renewals, silent failures caught before they affect payroll or onboarding.
  • Weekly labour review — beyond the automated Monday snapshot, a short written commentary identifying variance, overtime trends, and site-by-site outliers worth your attention.
  • Month-end reporting pack — labour vs revenue trends, overtime patterns, recruitment cost visibility, and a plain-English summary of what the numbers say about each site.
  • AI staff assistant tuning — new policy questions handled, edge cases added to the knowledge base, misrouted questions fixed, response quality reviewed weekly.
  • New automation credit — a monthly allocation for small new builds, tweaks, or improvements you request. Things that would otherwise be a separate project.
  • Compliance drift management — right-to-work renewals flagged, HR legislation changes reflected in the automation, contract templates kept current.
  • First-line support — direct WhatsApp/email access for managers when something's confusing or not behaving as expected. 24-hour response weekdays.
  • Quarterly strategy review — 60-minute call reviewing what's working, what's next, and where the next marginal hour of automation effort pays back.

Why this option matters for a three-site operator

A one-off build is a snapshot of today. Hospitality doesn't stand still — new starters arrive, policies change, platforms push updates, seasonal patterns shift. Without ongoing ownership, an automation system slowly degrades: the AI starts giving slightly wrong answers, the labour report stops matching the new rota software, and by month six someone in your team is back to doing it manually "just to be safe".

This option prevents that. The system stays current, and you have someone who knows the whole stack end-to-end on call when it matters. Think of it as the same relationship you have with your accountant — not a vendor you call when things break, but a partner who keeps things from breaking.

How we keep this safe

Automating HR and payroll adjacencies means handling sensitive data. These are the specific controls built into every option.

AI never invents numbers

This is non-negotiable. When a staff member asks "how much holiday do I have left?", the system retrieves their actual balance from BreatheHR via API and passes it to the AI as structured data. The AI's only job is to phrase the answer. It cannot guess, estimate, or make up numbers. If the data isn't available, it escalates to a human.

Payroll compliance stays with Xero

We do not touch RTI, HMRC submissions, tax codes, pension contributions, or statutory deductions. Xero handles all of that. Our automation feeds clean data in and reads clean data out — nothing in the compliance path is altered.

GDPR and data handling

All tools used (BreatheHR, Xero, Make.com, Claude, Twilio, Google Workspace) are GDPR-compliant with UK-legal data processing agreements. Staff data is never sent outside the EU/UK region. We document every data flow for your records.

Escalation paths

Every automation has a human fallback. The AI assistant escalates when unsure. Onboarding notifies a manager at each step. Labour alerts go to named individuals, not "the system". If anything breaks, you know immediately.

Off-switch

Every automation can be paused or disabled individually without taking down the whole system. Make.com gives per-scenario controls, and the AI assistant has a kill switch.
